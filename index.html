Story

The ancient Axiom Network, a machine-intelligence of unfathomable power, has begun to awaken from its millennia-long dormancy on the forgotten world of Taraxis Prime. Created by a civilization that predates even the Aeldari, the Axiom Network contains technological and arcane secrets that could fundamentally alter the balance of power across the galaxy.

The Robot Legion, driven by an obsession with mechanical perfection and perhaps guided by a deeper connection to the Network's origins, seeks to control this technology at any cost. However, as the Axiom Network stirs from its slumber, it has begun responding to the presence of various galactic forces, triggering reality-warping phenomena and corrupting the environment around its scattered nodes.

But rumors of the Axiom Network's power continue to spread, drawing more attention with each passing day. Imperial forces, xenos raiders, chaos cultists, and other powers great and small all have reason to seek control of—or destroy—this ancient technology.

Campaign Progress

In the first phase of this galactic struggle, intelligence pointed to a subterranean structure on Taraxis Prime—a tomb-like complex believed to house a crucial component of the Axiom Network. Multiple forces converged on this location, fighting through the planet's hostile conditions to claim the prize.

After a fierce battle among the ruins, the Human Defense Force secured the artifact—a crystalline matrix core pulsing with otherworldly energy. Initial analysis suggests it functions as some form of navigational key, potentially revealing the locations of other Axiom Network components scattered across the sector.

However, the excavation and removal of the artifact has triggered a cascade of strange phenomena across Taraxis Prime. Gravitational anomalies, temporal distortions, and instances of machine-spirits spontaneously achieving sentience have been reported. The Axiom Network appears to be responding to the removal of the component—perhaps even tracking it.

Missions

Overview

A mysterious signal emanates from an ancient and forgotten world. It leads to a tomb-like structure, deep beneath a planet's surface, said to be a precursor to the Mechanist Nexus. This ancient site is vital to unlocking the secrets of the Nexus, and now multiple factions have converged to claim it for their own purposes. The battle begins as factions scramble to unearth the tomb's entrance and secure its treasures before they are all lost.

Objective

Each faction races to claim the artifacts inside the tomb, which hold the key to accessing the Mechanist Nexus. The first team to capture and extract the Tomb Artifact from the center of the battlefield wins. The Tomb Artifact is a special objective marker that must be carried to a faction's deployment zone.

Special Rules

  • Tomb of Secrets: The Tomb Artifact is hidden in the center of the battlefield within a piece of dense terrain (such as a crumbling ruin or ancient control room). When any unit enters this terrain, they must roll a :
    • On a 4+, the player finds the Artifact and can pick it up.
    • On a 1-3, a random malfunction occurs (players roll a for a random event):
      1. Crumbling Terrain: The terrain becomes difficult to move through for the rest of the game.
      2. Tech Surge: A nearby machine becomes active and shoots at the unit that touched the artifact, dealing a damage to one model in that unit.
      3. Cloak of Darkness: The unit is disoriented and must reroll their next action with a -1 modifier.
  • No Reinforcements: For this mission, no reserve units can be called in. Players must fight with what they deploy at the start of the battle.
  • Neutral Forces: There are automated sentries patrolling the tomb, which will attack any unit (friend or foe) if they come within 6" of them. These sentries are treated as a neutral enemy force. They are not controlled by any player but will target the nearest unit, following normal targeting rules.

Terrain Suggestions

  • Shifting Ground: A large area of the board with unstable or shifting terrain that could block movement or line of sight at various points. This represents the volatile energy caused by the Nexus's awakening.
  • Damaged Power Relays: These relays (which are the Power Nodes) look like mechanical structures overrun with cables and flickering energy. They provide cover but also represent the unstable power of the Nexus.
  • Ruins of a Fallen City: Some areas of the battlefield are filled with wreckage from long-gone cities, where ancient technology remains active.

Deployment

Each player sets up their forces in their respective quarter. The center of the battlefield contains the "Tomb Artifact," an important objective marker located within the Ancient Control Room.

Victory Conditions

The first player to extract the Tomb Artifact to their deployment zone wins the mission.

  • Victory: The winning player gains access to the first piece of the Mechanist Nexus data and can upgrade one unit in their army (this could be a small, lore-based boost like better tech, weapons, or leadership abilities).
  • Defeat: The losing player suffers a significant delay in their plans, losing one unit permanently (a single unit is removed from their army list). The winner's success increases the likelihood of the Nexus activating earlier, which will influence the second mission.

Overview

The artifact recovered from the first mission has begun emitting coded pulses that point to a harsh, storm-ravaged planet. Intelligence suggests another Axiom Network component is located in an abandoned research outpost. Forces must navigate violent electrical storms that periodically disable technology while racing to extract the component from the deteriorating facility.

Objective

Primary Objective

Component Extraction: The Axiom component is locked away in a reinforced central chamber. A unit must first gather access codes from at least two of the three security terminals (by successfully activating them as detailed below) before entering the chamber and extracting the component. Once extracted, the unit must safely exit to the designated extraction zone.

Secondary Objectives

  • Data Recovery: Valuable historical data about the Axiom Network is stored in certain data terminals. Each such activation awards 1XP, representing strategic insights that may help later.
  • Storm Shielding: Some terminals control weather devices. When activated, they allow the player to designate one terrain piece to be shielded from the worst of the storm for one round.

Special Rules

  • Terminals: Place 9 objective markers representing terminals. When a unit reaches a terminal, roll a .
    • On a 1: The terminal is a Storm Control Terminal>. The player can activate a storm shield over one piece of terrain, granting cover and preventing units within from suffering storm penalties.
    • On a 2: The terminal is a Data Terminal. The player can download historical data, gaining 1 XP (or another narrative benefit).
    • On a 3: The terminal is a Security Terminal. The player gains a piece of coded data. Once a player has two coded data pieces, they can unlock the central chamber to retrieve the Axiom component.
  • Electrical Storm Surge: At the start of each round, roll a . On a 4+, the storm is active that round.
    • All units with the "Mechanical" or "Robot" quality suffer -1 to hit.
    • Energy weapons (laser, plasma, etc.) have their range halved.
    • Units in Sheltered Terrain (inside buildings, under intact structures) ignore the hit penalty from the storm.
    • Jump, Flyer, or Fast units take dangerous terrain tests (roll 1d6 for each model; on a 1, they take a wound).

Victory Conditions

A player wins immediately if they secure the Axiom component by unlocking the chamber and extracting it off the board (via an exit point or extraction action).

  • Victory: The winning player gains access to the second piece of the Axiom data and can upgrade one unit in their army with 1XP.
