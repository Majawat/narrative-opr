Story

An ancient alien machine, known only as the Mechanist Nexus, has begun to awaken from its long slumber on a forgotten world. It is said to hold secrets of both technological and arcane power that could shift the balance of the galaxy. The Robot Legion, driven by an obsession with their mechanical perfection, seeks to control the Nexus. However, the Nexus seems to be reacting to the presence of other forces in the galaxy, triggering strange phenomena and corrupting the environment around it.

Campaign Rules

[Here are where the specific rules we'll be following will be. Work in progress]

  • Campaign length is Game-Limited. We will play 10 games. Once each month starting in February and ending in November.
  • Army Sheet: Each army and unit must be given a unique name, which you can edit on your Army List.
  • Army Point Limits: there is no upper bound of available points, however no army may bring over 250pts more than the army with the lowest point value.
  • Underdog Bonus: Any army that has a point value lower than at least one other gets an underdog bonus. You get 1 underdog point for every 50pts difference between your army and the one with the highest value. Each underdog point can be spent to modify all dice in a friendly or enemy roll by +1 or -1, but only one point can be spent per roll.
  • Special Objectives: In addition to the mission objectives, players may earn a bonus of 75pts by completing special objectives. Roll one die each to see which objective you get:
    • Result Objective
      1 Mastery - One friendly unit in each table quarter at the end.
      2 Honor - No enemy units in own deployment zone at the end.
      3 Casting - At least one attempt to cast a spell per round.
      4 Recovery - First player to stop a unit from being shaken.
      5 Rage - First player to destroy an enemy unit.
      6 Destruction - Most expensive enemy unit destroyed.
    • If you roll an objective which can't be completed, then you must re-roll.

Missions

Overview

A mysterious signal emanates from an ancient and forgotten world. It leads to a tomb-like structure, deep beneath a planet's surface, said to be a precursor to the Mechanist Nexus. This ancient site is vital to unlocking the secrets of the Nexus, and now multiple factions have converged to claim it for their own purposes. The battle begins as factions scramble to unearth the tomb's entrance and secure its treasures before they are all lost.

Objective

Each faction races to claim the artifacts inside the tomb, which hold the key to accessing the Mechanist Nexus. The first team to capture and extract the Tomb Artifact from the center of the battlefield wins. The Tomb Artifact is a special objective marker that must be carried to a faction's deployment zone.

Special Rules

  • Tomb of Secrets: The Tomb Artifact is hidden in the center of the battlefield within a piece of dense terrain (such as a crumbling ruin or ancient control room). When any unit enters this terrain, they must roll a D6:
    • On a 4+, the player finds the Artifact and can pick it up.
    • On a 1-3, a random malfunction occurs (players roll a D6 for a random event):
      1. Crumbling Terrain: The terrain becomes difficult to move through for the rest of the game.
      2. Tech Surge: A nearby machine becomes active and shoots at the unit that touched the artifact, dealing a D6 damage to one model in that unit.
      3. Cloak of Darkness: The unit is disoriented and must reroll their next action with a -1 modifier.
  • No Reinforcements: For this mission, no reserve units can be called in. Players must fight with what they deploy at the start of the battle.
  • Neutral Forces: There are automated sentries patrolling the tomb, which will attack any unit (friend or foe) if they come within 6" of them. These sentries are treated as a neutral enemy force. They are not controlled by any player but will target the nearest unit, following normal targeting rules.

Terrain Suggestions

  • Shifting Ground: A large area of the board with unstable or shifting terrain that could block movement or line of sight at various points. This represents the volatile energy caused by the Nexus's awakening.
  • Damaged Power Relays: These relays (which are the Power Nodes) look like mechanical structures overrun with cables and flickering energy. They provide cover but also represent the unstable power of the Nexus.
  • Ruins of a Fallen City: Some areas of the battlefield are filled with wreckage from long-gone cities, where ancient technology remains active.

Deployment

Each player sets up their forces in their respective quarter. The center of the battlefield contains the "Tomb Artifact," an important objective marker located within the Ancient Control Room.

Victory Conditions

The first player to extract the Tomb Artifact to their deployment zone wins the mission.

  • Victory: The winning player gains access to the first piece of the Mechanist Nexus data and can upgrade one unit in their army (this could be a small, lore-based boost like better tech, weapons, or leadership abilities).
  • Defeat: The losing player suffers a significant delay in their plans, losing one unit permanently (a single unit is removed from their army list). The winner's success increases the likelihood of the Nexus activating earlier, which will influence the second mission.

Overview

The Tomb Artifact has been secured, but the action has caused the ancient mechanisms of the tomb to stir to life, triggering environmental chaos. The Nexus is beginning to wake, and dangerous, volatile terrain has begun to affect the battlefield. The factions are forced to confront one another in a skirmish, as the storm of mechanical and environmental chaos grows stronger.

Objective

The factions must control specific locations that are connected to the Nexus activation. These are Power Nodes that fuel the activation process. Each faction wants to control them to prevent their enemies from accessing them, or to secure their position for future advantage.
